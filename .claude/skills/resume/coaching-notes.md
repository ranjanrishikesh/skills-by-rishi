# Coaching Notes — Phase 7 Reference

The resume is one artifact in a larger job-search system. This phase connects resume improvements to the broader strategy and gives the user a concrete action plan for what to do NEXT.

---

## Structure

Deliver exactly 3-5 actionable next steps. Each must be:
- **Specific** (not "improve your LinkedIn" but "rewrite your LinkedIn headline to match: [exact text]")
- **Prioritized** (numbered by impact)
- **Connected to findings** (reference which Review, Devil's Advocate, or Architecture finding it addresses)

---

## Coaching Categories

Pick 3-5 from these categories based on what's most relevant to the user's Goal Profile:

### 1. Portfolio & Case Studies
When to include: Always for designers, PMs, and anyone whose work is visual or process-heavy.

Specific advice:
- Which 2-3 projects from the resume deserve full case studies?
- What should each case study cover? (Problem → Process → Solution → Results)
- What's missing from the current portfolio link (if provided)?
- How should case studies be structured to mirror the resume's narrative?
- Should they create a portfolio site if they don't have one?

### 2. LinkedIn Alignment
When to include: Always — LinkedIn is the #1 place recruiters verify resume claims.

Specific advice:
- Headline should match the resume's target positioning (provide exact suggested text)
- Summary should expand on the resume summary (provide a draft or outline)
- Experience bullets should be LONGER on LinkedIn than on resume (LinkedIn rewards detail)
- Skills section should include all keywords from the resume plus additional ones
- Recommendations to request: from whom, saying what
- Content strategy: what to post/share to reinforce positioning (1-2 specific ideas)

### 3. Networking Moves
When to include: When the goal involves targeting specific companies or breaking into a new field.

Specific advice:
- Who to reach out to (role types, not specific people)
- What to say in outreach messages (provide a template or framework)
- Communities, Slack groups, or events relevant to the target role/industry
- Informational interview strategy: what to ask, how to follow up
- How to leverage the user's existing network for warm introductions

### 4. Skills to Acquire or Certify
When to include: When the Review or Devil's Advocate identified skill gaps that can't be papered over.

Specific advice:
- Prioritized list of 1-3 skills to learn (ranked by ROI for the goal)
- Specific resources: courses, certifications, projects to build
- Timeline: realistic estimate for getting proficient enough to list on resume
- How to demonstrate the skill before formal experience (side projects, open source, volunteering)
- Which certifications actually matter for the target role vs. which are resume filler

### 5. Interview Prep Focus Areas
When to include: Always — the resume's weak spots predict interview challenges.

Specific advice:
- The 3-5 hardest questions they'll face based on resume weak spots (from Phase 4 Hiring Manager persona)
- How to frame each answer using the STAR method
- Stories to prepare: which resume bullets will they be asked to expand on?
- Questions to prepare for gaps, transitions, or short tenures
- How to proactively address the Devil's Advocate's "uncomfortable truth" if it comes up

### 6. Application Strategy
When to include: When the user is in active job search mode (Goals A or C).

Specific advice:
- How to customize this resume for different applications (what to change vs. keep static)
- Cover letter strategy: key themes to hit for the target role
- Application timing and channel strategy (direct apply vs. referral vs. recruiter)
- How many applications to send and where to focus energy
- Red flags in job postings to watch for

### 7. Personal Brand & Thought Leadership
When to include: For senior roles, freelance positioning, or career pivoters who need to build credibility in a new space.

Specific advice:
- Content to create: blog posts, talks, or projects that reinforce the target positioning
- Platforms to be active on (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Medium, Dribbble, etc.)
- Topics to own: 1-2 themes to become known for
- How to document and share work publicly

---

## Format

Present as a numbered, prioritized action list:

```
### Next Steps — Beyond the Resume

**1. [Most impactful action] (Priority: Critical)**
[2-3 sentences of specific advice]
_This addresses: [Reference to Phase 3/4 finding]_

**2. [Second action] (Priority: High)**
[2-3 sentences of specific advice]
_This addresses: [Reference to Phase 3/4 finding]_

**3. [Third action] (Priority: Medium)**
...

[Up to 5 items maximum]
```

---

## Closing

End with a single paragraph that:
- Acknowledges the user's strengths (genuine, not patronizing)
- Reframes the biggest weakness as an opportunity
- Connects back to the stated goal
- Leaves them with clear momentum — they should know exactly what to do next
