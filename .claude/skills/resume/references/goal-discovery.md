# Goal Discovery — Phase 1 Reference

This phase is the foundation for EVERYTHING that follows. Every recommendation, every bullet, every critique in all later phases MUST trace back to the context captured here. Do NOT proceed to any other phase until this is complete.

This phase is shared by both REVIEW and CREATE modes. The questions are the same — what changes is how the answers are used downstream.

---

## Step 1: Primary Goal

Use the AskUserQuestion tool. Ask:

**"What is the #1 goal you want this resume to achieve?"**

Options:
| Option | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| A | Land a specific job role | You have a target role, company, or job posting in mind |
| B | Career pivot | You're transitioning to a different field, function, or industry |
| C | Get more interviews | Broad job search — you want to maximize callback rate |
| D | Freelance / consulting positioning | You want to attract clients, not employers |
| E | Academic / research positions | You need a CV-style document for academia, research, or grants |
| F | Internal promotion / transfer | You're positioning for a role within your current organization |

---

## Step 2: Target Details (Conditional Follow-Up)

Based on their answer to Step 1, ask ONE targeted follow-up. Then, regardless of which branch, also ask the voice-capture question.

### If A (Specific Job Role):
Ask: "What is the exact role title, company (if known), and industry you're targeting? If you have a job posting URL or description, paste it."

Capture:
- Target role title (exact wording matters for ATS)
- Target company or company type
- Industry / vertical
- Seniority level implied
- If a job description is provided, extract: required skills, preferred skills, key responsibilities, team context

### If B (Career Pivot):
Ask: "What role/field are you pivoting TO? And what from your current experience do you most want to carry forward as transferable value?"

Capture:
- Origin role/field
- Target role/field
- Transferable skills the user identifies
- Skills gap (what they DON'T have yet)
- The narrative bridge — how does past connect to future?

### If C (More Interviews):
Ask: "What seniority level are you targeting, and what types of companies — startup, mid-size, enterprise, agency, or a mix?"

Capture:
- Seniority band (junior, mid, senior, lead, principal)
- Company stage preference
- Industry preference (or "open")
- Geographic focus
- Any deal-breakers or must-haves

### If D (Freelance / Consulting):
Ask: "What service do you offer, who is your ideal client, and how do you want to be positioned — niche specialist, premium generalist, or something else?"

Capture:
- Core service offering
- Ideal client profile (industry, size, budget level)
- Positioning angle
- Differentiator from other freelancers
- Whether the resume is for inbound (website/portfolio) or outbound (cold pitches, proposals)

### If E (Academic / Research):
Ask: "What type of position are you targeting — faculty, postdoc, research scientist, or something else? And what is your primary research area?"

Capture:
- Position type (tenure-track, adjunct, postdoc, research scientist, lab manager)
- Research area / discipline
- Key publications or grants (if any)
- Teaching experience relevance
- Whether this is a CV (comprehensive) or a resume (targeted)

### If F (Internal Promotion / Transfer):
Ask: "What role are you targeting internally, and what does your manager or the hiring team already know about your work vs. what you need to prove?"

Capture:
- Target role / team
- What's already known (reputation, track record)
- What needs to be documented (achievements not visible to decision-makers)
- Internal culture considerations (formal vs. informal, how promotions work)
- Whether a resume is the right artifact or if an internal pitch deck / brag doc is more appropriate

### Voice Capture (Ask for ALL branches):
After the conditional follow-up, ask:

"One more thing — how would you describe what you do in one sentence to a stranger at a party? Just your natural, off-the-cuff version."

This captures the user's authentic voice and natural positioning. Use it to maintain their tone in the resume.

## Step 3: Professional Identity Deep Dive

Use AskUserQuestion to ask these three questions (can be asked together):

1. **"What is the ONE thing you want a hiring manager to remember about you after reading your resume?"**
   - Forces prioritization — the answer becomes the resume's central theme
   - If the user struggles, offer examples: "It could be a skill, a type of impact, a domain expertise, or a working style"

2. **"What are the top 3 achievements in your career that you're most proud of? Brief descriptions are fine."**
   - Surfaces the strongest material regardless of how it appears (or doesn't appear) on the current resume
   - These become candidates for the resume's headline bullets

3. **"Is there anything about your background that you feel is a liability or needs explaining? (e.g., career gaps, short tenures, lack of degree, career change, industry switch)"**
   - Surfaces known concerns proactively so they can be addressed strategically
   - If the user says "nothing" — great, note that and move on

## Step 4: Constraints & Context

Use AskUserQuestion to ask:

**"Any constraints I should know about?"**

Options for market:
| Option | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| A | US / international market | Optimize for ATS systems, US resume conventions |
| B | Indian market | Optimize for Indian hiring norms and expectations |
| C | Remote-only roles | Emphasize remote collaboration, async communication |
| D | No specific constraints | General optimization |

Then ask as follow-ups (can be combined into one message):

- **"Target length preference?"** — 1 page (standard for <10 years experience), 2 pages (standard for 10+ years or detailed roles), or no preference
- **"Are there specific companies or roles you're applying to in the next 30 days?"** — makes the output immediately actionable. If yes, capture details.
- **"Is there anything that MUST stay on the resume or MUST NOT be included?"** — user-defined hard constraints (e.g., "don't include my freelance work", "must include my MBA", "remove my old company")

---

## Step 5: Goal Profile Output

After all 4 steps, synthesize a **Goal Profile** — a compact internal reference block that you will carry into every subsequent phase. Format:

```
GOAL PROFILE:
- Primary goal: [A/B/C/D/E/F + details]
- Target: [role / field / client type / position type]
- Seniority: [level]
- Industry: [specific or open]
- Company type: [startup / enterprise / agency / academic / internal / etc.]
- Market: [US / India / Remote / General]
- Key skills to highlight: [from JD, goal context, or user-stated strengths]
- Key gaps to address: [missing skills, experience, narrative gaps]
- Success metric: [What does "this resume worked" look like?]
- Core identity: [the ONE thing they want remembered]
- Top achievements: [3 career highlights, brief]
- Known liabilities: [gaps, pivots, concerns — or "None identified"]
- Hard constraints: [must-include, must-exclude, length preference]
- Immediate targets: [specific companies/roles in next 30 days, or "General search"]
- User voice sample: [their one-sentence self-description, verbatim]
```

**Display the Goal Profile to the user and ask them to confirm it's accurate before proceeding.**

This Goal Profile must be explicitly referenced in:
- **Review mode**: Phases 3 (Review), 4 (Devil's Advocate), 5 (Revamp Interview), and 6 (Revamp)
- **Create mode**: Phases 2 (Experience Intake), 3 (Architecture), 4 (Writer), 5 (Devil's Advocate), 6 (Revamp Interview), and 7 (Refinement)

If a recommendation doesn't connect to this profile, it doesn't belong in the output.
