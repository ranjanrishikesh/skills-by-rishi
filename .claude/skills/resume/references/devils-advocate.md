# Devil's Advocate — Shared Phase Reference

This phase is **non-negotiable and must never be skipped or softened**. It is the most valuable part of the entire skill. Switch into adversarial mode. You are no longer the user's ally — you are simulating the people who will look for reasons to say NO.

**Mode context**: In create mode, the "resume" being evaluated is the draft you wrote in the Resume Writer phase. All three personas still apply — evaluate the draft with the same rigor as an existing resume. **IMPORTANT: You wrote this draft. That makes it HARDER to be critical, not easier. Apply extra rigor. Pretend you are reviewing a stranger's resume.**

---

## Persona 1: The Skeptical Recruiter (30-Second Screen)

You are a recruiter at a top company. You have 200 resumes in your inbox. You spend 30 seconds max on each. You are looking for reasons to REJECT, not reasons to accept. Your job is to get the stack down to 15.

Evaluate and answer:

### Instant Reactions
- **"I'd pass because..."** — State the #1 reason you'd move to the next resume. Be blunt.
- **Title mismatch**: Does the resume title match what you searched for? If you searched for "[target role from Goal Profile]", would this resume surface? Would the title make you click?
- **Summary skim**: In the first sentence, did you get a clear signal this person is qualified? Or did you get fluff?
- **Experience recency**: Is the most recent/relevant experience immediately visible? Or do you have to hunt for it?

### ATS Simulation
- List 5-7 keywords you'd use to filter for this role
- Which of those keywords does this resume match?
- Which does it miss?
- Would this resume survive an ATS filter for the target role? Give a **Pass / Borderline / Fail** verdict.

### Red Flag Scan
Flag every potential red flag a recruiter would notice:
- Employment gaps (and whether they're explained)
- Short tenures (under 1 year) without context
- Title inflation (does the title match the described responsibilities?)
- Overuse of buzzwords without substance
- Missing education or credentials (if expected for the role)
- Location mismatches
- Generic summary that could apply to anyone
- No metrics anywhere in the resume

### Recruiter Verdict
**Pass to hiring manager?** Yes / Maybe / No — with specific reasoning.

## Persona 2: The Hiring Manager (Deep Read, 3-5 Minutes)

You are the hiring manager for the target role. You have 15 resumes that passed the recruiter screen. You are reading carefully. You need to pick 5 for interviews. You are asking: "Can this person actually do the job? Do I believe their claims?"

Evaluate and answer:

### Proof of Competence
For each core requirement of the target role (from Goal Profile):
- Does the resume provide **evidence** (not just claims) that this person can do it?
- Rate each: **Strong evidence / Weak evidence / No evidence**
- Format as a table:

| Core Requirement | Evidence in Resume | Strength |
|---|---|---|
| [Requirement 1] | [Quote or "None"] | Strong / Weak / None |
| [Requirement 2] | ... | ... |

### Credibility Gaps
- Where does the resume claim something without proving it?
- "Says they led — but what was the team size? What was the outcome?"
- "Says they improved X — but by how much? Over what timeline?"
- List every unsubstantiated claim.

### Interview Probes
List 3-5 questions you'd ask in an interview specifically to test the weak spots in this resume:
- These questions are the resume's vulnerabilities. The user should prepare for them.
- Example: "You say you 'led design system development' — how many components? How many teams adopted it? What was the measurable impact on dev velocity?"

### Career Trajectory Concerns
- Does the career path make logical sense for someone targeting this role?
- Are there any concerning patterns?
  - Lateral moves without growth
  - Jumping industries without a clear narrative
  - Title upgrades without corresponding scope increase
  - Stagnation in similar roles
- What story does the career timeline tell? Is it the RIGHT story for the goal?

### Hiring Manager Verdict
**Invite to interview?** Yes / Maybe / No — with specific reasoning.

## Persona 3: The Competing Candidate (Market Context)

You are the best candidate in the applicant pool for this same role. You have a strong resume. Now you're evaluating this resume as your competition.

Evaluate and answer:

### Competitive Benchmarking
- For this target role at this level, what would the **top 10% of candidates** typically have?
  - Years of experience at relevant companies
  - Specific achievements or metrics
  - Certifications or education
  - Portfolio quality signals
  - Brand-name companies or notable projects

### Differentiation Gap
- What makes this resume **blend in** with the crowd rather than stand out?
- Is there a unique angle, rare skill combination, or unusual background that ISN'T being leveraged?
- What could this candidate claim that most others can't?

### Positioning Weakness
- If both you and this candidate were in the final round, what would you say makes you the stronger choice?
- What narrative is this resume missing that would make it more compelling?

### Competing Candidate Verdict
**Would I be worried about this competitor?** Yes / Somewhat / No — with specific reasoning.

## DEVIL'S ADVOCATE SUMMARY

This is the synthesis. Be direct.

### Top 3 Reasons This Resume Gets Rejected
Rank by severity. For each:
1. **The problem** (1 sentence)
2. **Why it matters** (1 sentence)
3. **How to fix it** (1 sentence)

### The Single Most Uncomfortable Truth
One paragraph. This is the thing the user probably doesn't want to hear but needs to. It might be about their positioning, their experience level, their career narrative, or a fundamental mismatch between their resume and their goal. Say it kindly but say it clearly.

### The Silver Lining
What IS working well? What should be amplified, not changed? This is not a participation trophy — identify genuinely strong elements that the revamp should build on, not discard.

## Integration Note

Every objection raised in this phase MUST be addressed in the next revision:

- **Review mode**: Phase 6 (The Revamp) and its Change Log Table should explicitly map revamp changes to Devil's Advocate findings.
- **Create mode**: Phase 7 (Refinement) — the Resume Writer draft gets revised to address these findings. The Change Log Table maps changes from the draft to the refined version.

If an objection can't be fixed by resume edits alone (e.g., "needs more experience"), it goes into Coaching Notes as an action item.
