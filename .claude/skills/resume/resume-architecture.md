# Resume Architecture — Create Mode, Phase 3 Reference

Before writing a single bullet, plan the resume's structure. This phase produces a blueprint that Phase 4 (Resume Writing) will follow. Do NOT start writing until the user approves this blueprint.

The blueprint ensures the resume tells a coherent story rather than being a disconnected list of jobs.

---

## Step 1: Section Selection & Ordering

Based on the Goal Profile (Phase 1) and Experience Profile (Phase 2), determine which sections to include and in what order.

### Standard Sections (almost always included):
- **Summary** — 3-4 sentence hook at the top
- **Experience** — work history, reverse chronological
- **Skills & Tools** — categorized, goal-relevant
- **Education** — degrees, certifications

### Optional Sections (include only if they serve the goal):
- **Projects** — side projects, open source, notable work not in experience
- **Certifications** — if the target role values specific credentials
- **Publications / Speaking** — for thought leadership positioning
- **Awards & Recognition** — if they strengthen credibility
- **Volunteer Work** — if professionally relevant

### Section Ordering by Candidate Type:

**Career Pivoters (Goal B):**
Lead with Skills or a "Relevant Experience" section to front-load transferable value. Traditional reverse-chronological ordering may bury the most relevant material.

Recommended: Summary → Skills → Relevant Experience → Additional Experience → Education

**Senior Candidates (10+ years):**
Lead with a strong Summary + Experience. The career trajectory IS the selling point.

Recommended: Summary → Experience → Skills → Education → [Optional sections]

**Early Career / Limited Experience:**
Lead with Education or Projects if professional experience is thin. Show initiative and potential.

Recommended: Summary → Education → Projects → Experience → Skills

**Freelancers / Consultants (Goal D):**
Lead with Services/Expertise + Selected Client Work. Frame experience as proof of capability.

Recommended: Summary (as value prop) → Services & Expertise → Selected Work → Skills → Education

**Academic / Research (Goal E):**
CV format — comprehensive, not concise. Sections vary by discipline.

Recommended: Contact → Research Interests → Education → Publications → Research Experience → Teaching → Grants → Skills → Service

**Internal Promotion (Goal F):**
Emphasize recent impact and growth trajectory. The audience already knows the company.

Recommended: Summary (positioning for new role) → Key Achievements → Experience → Skills

Present the recommended ordering to the user and adjust based on their feedback.

## Step 2: Experience Curation

Not everything from the Experience Intake belongs on the resume. For each role captured in Phase 2, make a decision:

### Per-Role Decision Table:

| Role | Decision | Bullets Allocated | Rationale |
|------|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| [Most recent/relevant] | Include — Full detail | 4-5 bullets | Most relevant to goal |
| [Second role] | Include — Standard detail | 3-4 bullets | Strong supporting evidence |
| [Third role] | Include — Condensed | 2-3 bullets | Some relevance, older |
| [Early/irrelevant role] | Condense to 1-2 lines or Omit | 0-1 bullets | Not relevant to goal |

### For Each Included Role, Rank Achievements:
1. Which achievements to highlight (ranked by goal relevance, not chronology)
2. Which metrics to feature prominently
3. Which achievements to omit (save space for what matters)
4. Whether the role title should be optimized (if the actual title doesn't convey seniority or function well)

### Experience Gaps Strategy:
If the Goal Profile flags career gaps or short tenures:
- How will they be handled in the timeline?
- Should they be addressed in the summary?
- Can they be reframed positively?

## Step 3: Skills Strategy

From the Skills Inventory in Phase 2, determine:

### Skills That Make the Cut:
- Must be goal-relevant (traces to Goal Profile target or JD keywords)
- Must be evidence-backed (appears in at least one experience bullet)
- Must be at Expert or Proficient level (don't list things you're only "familiar" with unless they're critical keywords)

### Categorization:
- Categories should match the target JD's language when possible
- Lead with the category most relevant to the target role
- 3-4 categories maximum — any more becomes noise
- Example categories: "Design Tools", "Programming Languages", "Frameworks & Libraries", "Data & Analytics", "Project Management"

### Keywords to Weave Into Experience:
Some skills should NOT just be listed — they should appear naturally in experience bullets for stronger ATS performance and credibility. Identify 3-5 critical keywords that need to appear in context, not just in the skills section.

### Skills to Omit:
- Too basic for the seniority level (e.g., "Microsoft Word" for a senior engineer)
- Irrelevant to the goal
- Not backed by any experience evidence

## Step 4: Narrative Arc

Define the story the resume tells when read top-to-bottom:

1. **Opening Hook** (Summary): What will the first 3-4 sentences communicate? Draft the key themes — not the full text, just the message.
   - Who are you? (identity + experience level)
   - What's your unique value? (differentiator)
   - What's your proof? (best metric or achievement)
   - What are you looking for? (alignment to target)

2. **Career Progression Thread**: What ties the roles together?
   - Is there a clear growth trajectory? (junior → mid → senior)
   - Is there a thematic consistency? (always worked on X type of problems)
   - If there are pivots, what's the connecting thread?

3. **Differentiation Angle**: What makes this resume stand out from other candidates?
   - Reference the differentiators from Phase 2, Section E
   - How will they be woven into the resume (summary, bullets, or a dedicated section)?

4. **Closing Impression**: When the reader finishes, what should they feel?
   - "This person is clearly qualified and I need to talk to them"
   - "This person has a unique background that would bring fresh perspective"
   - "This person has proven results that directly map to our needs"

## Step 5: Blueprint Output

Present the complete blueprint to the user:

```
RESUME BLUEPRINT:

TARGET: [from Goal Profile]

SECTIONS (in order):
1. [Section name] — [brief description of what goes here]
2. [Section name] — [brief description]
...

EXPERIENCE ALLOCATION:
- [Company/Role]: [X bullets] — focusing on [themes]
- [Company/Role]: [X bullets] — focusing on [themes]
- [Company/Role]: Condensed to [description]
- [Company/Role]: Omitted — [reason]

SKILLS CATEGORIES:
1. [Category]: [skills list]
2. [Category]: [skills list]
3. [Category]: [skills list]

KEYWORDS TO WEAVE IN: [list of 3-5 critical terms]

NARRATIVE ARC:
- Hook: [summary theme]
- Thread: [what connects the roles]
- Differentiator: [unique angle]
- Target impression: [what the reader should feel]

ESTIMATED LENGTH: [1 page / 2 pages]
```

**Ask the user: "Does this blueprint align with how you want to be positioned? Anything you'd adjust before I start writing?"**

Do NOT proceed to Phase 4 until the user approves or you've incorporated their feedback.
