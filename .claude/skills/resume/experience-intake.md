# Experience Intake — Create Mode, Phase 2 Reference

This phase replaces Resume Ingestion. Since there is no existing resume, you must build a complete picture of the user's professional history through structured questioning. The quality of the final resume depends entirely on the depth of information gathered here.

---

## Approach

Do NOT dump all questions at once. Work through sections one at a time. After each section, summarize what you captured and confirm with the user before moving on. This prevents information overload and gives the user space to think deeply about each area.

Use the Goal Profile from Phase 1 to guide your probing — spend more time on areas most relevant to the user's target.

---

## Section A: Work History (Most Recent First)

Ask: "Let's start with your work history. Beginning with your most recent (or current) role, tell me about each position you've held. For each role, I need:
- Company name and what they do (1 sentence is fine)
- Your job title
- Start and end dates (approximate is fine)
- 3-5 things you did or accomplished in that role

Start with your most recent role — we'll work backwards from there."

**For EACH role the user provides, probe deeper with these follow-ups:**

1. **Team & Scope**: "What was the team size and your reporting structure? Did you manage anyone? Were you a sole contributor or part of a larger team?"
2. **Biggest Impact**: "What was the single biggest impact you had in this role? The thing that, if you could only mention one accomplishment, you'd choose?"
3. **Metrics**: "Can you attach any numbers to your work? Users impacted, revenue influenced, time saved, percentage improvements, team size, scale of systems, anything quantifiable — even rough estimates."
4. **Tools & Technologies**: "What tools, technologies, frameworks, or methodologies did you use daily in this role?"
5. **Transition Context**: "Why did you leave? (Or why are you looking to leave, if current)" — helps with narrative continuity

**Keep going until the user has covered all relevant roles.** For very early-career or irrelevant roles, a brief summary is fine — but always ask if there's anything from those roles worth highlighting.

**After completing all roles, summarize:**

```
WORK HISTORY CAPTURED:
1. [Company] — [Title] ([Dates])
   Key achievements: [brief list]
   Metrics: [any numbers captured]

2. [Company] — [Title] ([Dates])
   ...
```

Confirm with the user: "Does this look right? Anything I missed or got wrong?"

---

## Section B: Education & Certifications

Ask: "What's your educational background? Include:
- Degrees (institution, field of study, graduation year)
- Relevant certifications (name, issuing body, year)
- Notable coursework, thesis, or research projects (only if recent or highly relevant to your goal)"

Capture:
- Degree(s) with full details
- Certifications with dates
- Any academic honors or distinctions
- Relevance to the target role (flag if education is a strong asset or a non-factor)

---

## Section C: Skills Inventory

Ask: "List all the skills and tools you're proficient in. Don't self-filter — list everything, and I'll help you decide what makes the cut for the resume."

After the user provides their list, categorize them together:

**Expert level** (could teach others, deep hands-on experience):
- [skills]

**Proficient** (use regularly and confidently in professional work):
- [skills]

**Familiar** (have used, could ramp up quickly if needed):
- [skills]

Then cross-reference against the Goal Profile:
- Which skills are critical for the target role?
- Which skills are nice-to-have?
- Which skills are irrelevant and should be omitted?
- Are there any skills the user SHOULD have but didn't mention? (flag as a gap)

---

## Section D: Projects & Side Work

Ask: "Any notable work outside of your day jobs? This includes:
- Side projects or personal projects
- Open source contributions
- Freelance or consulting work
- Volunteer work with professional relevance
- Publications, blog posts, talks, or conference presentations
- Teaching or mentoring (formal or informal)

Only mention things that either demonstrate skills relevant to your goal or show initiative and depth."

For each notable item, capture:
- What it was
- Your role / contribution
- Any measurable outcome or recognition
- How it relates to the target role

---

## Section E: Unique Differentiators

Ask: "Last section — what makes you different from other people with a similar background? Think about:
- Unusual skill combinations (e.g., engineering + design, finance + product)
- Non-obvious experiences that gave you a unique perspective
- Things people consistently come to you for help with
- Recognition, awards, or notable feedback you've received
- Any unique context about your career path that sets you apart"

This section surfaces the differentiators that prevent the resume from sounding like everyone else's. Even small details can be powerful positioning tools.

---

## Experience Profile Output

After all 5 sections are complete, compile an **Experience Profile** — a structured summary of everything captured. This is the raw material for Phase 3 (Architecture) and Phase 4 (Writing).

```
EXPERIENCE PROFILE:

WORK HISTORY:
[Role 1]
- Company: [name] — [what they do]
- Title: [title]
- Dates: [start] – [end]
- Key Achievements: [bulleted list with metrics where available]
- Tools/Tech: [list]
- Team Context: [size, structure, management scope]

[Role 2]
...

EDUCATION:
- [Degree, Institution, Year]
- Certifications: [list]

SKILLS (by proficiency):
- Expert: [list]
- Proficient: [list]
- Familiar: [list]
- Goal-critical skills: [subset most relevant to target]
- Skills gaps: [missing skills for target role]

PROJECTS & SIDE WORK:
- [Project 1]: [description, role, outcome]
- ...

DIFFERENTIATORS:
- [Unique angle 1]
- [Unique angle 2]
- [What people come to them for]
- [Recognition/awards]

NARRATIVE NOTES:
- Career arc: [brief description of progression]
- Transition contexts: [why they moved between roles]
- Liabilities to address: [from Goal Profile]
- Strongest material: [top 3-5 items across all sections]
```

**Display the Experience Profile to the user and confirm accuracy before proceeding to Phase 3.**
