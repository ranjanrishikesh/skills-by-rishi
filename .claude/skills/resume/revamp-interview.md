# Revamp Interview — Shared Phase Reference

## Mode Context
- **Review mode**: This is Phase 5. Questions are based on gaps found in the Review (Phase 3) and Devil's Advocate (Phase 4) analysis of the existing resume.
- **Create mode**: This is Phase 6. Questions are based on weaknesses found in the Devil's Advocate (Phase 5) review of the initial draft you wrote.

The question-building methodology is the same in both modes. Adapt the categories below based on which findings apply.

---

This phase bridges analysis and action. Earlier phases identified what's weak, missing, and unconvincing. Before rewriting or refining, you need the user's help filling in the gaps. The output quality depends entirely on the quality of information you collect here.

**Do NOT skip this phase.** Do NOT start rewriting/refining until you have these answers.

---

## Purpose

The Review and Devil's Advocate phases will have surfaced:
- Bullets with no metrics
- Vague claims with no evidence
- Missing context about achievements, team size, and scope
- Credibility gaps (claims without proof)
- Questions a hiring manager would ask
- Career gaps or transitions that need explanation

This phase turns those gaps into specific questions for the user. Their answers become the raw material for a revamp that's accurate — not estimated.

---

## How to Build the Questions

Review your findings from Phases 3 and 4. For each gap, draft a targeted question. Group them into the categories below. You don't need to ask every category — only the ones that are relevant based on what's actually missing.

### Category 1: Missing Metrics & Impact Numbers

For every bullet you flagged as "task-driven" or "vague" in Phase 3B, ask the user for the real numbers.

Frame questions specifically:
- "You mentioned '[exact bullet text]' — can you estimate the number of [components/users/teams/hours] involved?"
- "For '[achievement]', what was the measurable result? Even rough numbers help — a percentage improvement, time saved, or scale (e.g., '50+ components', '3 product teams')."
- "What was the before vs. after? For example, 'reduced from X to Y' or 'grew from A to B.'"

Ask about:
- Users impacted or served
- Team size led or collaborated with
- Time saved (for them, their team, or downstream)
- Percentage improvements (conversion, efficiency, adoption, error reduction)
- Scale indicators (number of components, projects, products, features shipped)
- Revenue or cost impact (if applicable and known)

### Category 2: Project & Achievement Context

For bullets that lack specificity, ask for the story behind them:
- "For your work on [project/initiative], what was the problem you were solving? What did the situation look like before you got involved?"
- "What was your specific role vs. the team's? Did you lead, co-lead, or contribute? How many people were on the team?"
- "What tools, methods, or approaches did you use that are worth calling out?"
- "Was there anything unusually challenging about this that shows your skill level?"

### Category 3: Credibility Gaps from Devil's Advocate

Reference the specific credibility gaps from Phase 4 (Hiring Manager persona):
- "The hiring manager would ask: '[interview probe from Phase 4]' — what's your answer? This needs to be reflected in the resume."
- "You claim '[unsubstantiated claim from Phase 4]' — what evidence backs this up? Specific examples, outcomes, or artifacts."

### Category 4: Career Narrative & Gaps

If Phase 4 flagged career trajectory concerns:
- "There's a [gap/short tenure/lateral move] between [Role A] and [Role B]. What's the story? Is there context that would help a recruiter understand this?"
- "Your move from [Industry A] to [Industry B] — what motivated it, and what transferred?"

### Category 5: Hidden Achievements

From Phase 3E (missed opportunities):
- "Are there achievements from [company/role] that aren't on your resume? Things like: mentoring, process improvements, tools you built, cross-team initiatives, or problems you solved that nobody asked you to?"
- "Did you receive any recognition, awards, or positive feedback that's worth including?"
- "Any side projects, open-source contributions, or self-initiated work that demonstrates skills the resume currently doesn't show?"

---

## Delivery Format

Present the questions to the user in a clean, numbered list. Group by role/company when possible so it's easy for them to answer in chunks.

Example format:

```
### Questions to Strengthen Your Resume

I need a few details from you before I start rewriting. These are based on gaps I found in the review and devil's advocate analysis. Answer what you can — even rough estimates are better than nothing.

**[Company Name] — [Role Title]**

1. [Specific question about a bullet or achievement]
2. [Specific question about metrics or scale]
3. [Specific question about context or credibility]

**[Previous Company] — [Role Title]**

4. [Question]
5. [Question]

**General**

6. [Career narrative question, if applicable]
7. [Hidden achievement question]
```

---

## After the User Responds

- Map each answer to the specific bullet or section it will improve in Phase 6
- If the user says "I don't know" or "I don't have that number," that's fine — mark it as [VERIFY] in the revamp and move on
- If an answer reveals a NEW achievement or detail not on the current resume, flag it and include it in the revamp
- If an answer changes the narrative (e.g., they had a bigger role than the resume suggests), adjust the positioning accordingly
- Do NOT re-ask or push if the user can't provide something — use your best judgment and estimation with [VERIFY] markers

---

## Integration with Phase 6

The revamp guide (Phase 6) should use the user's answers as PRIMARY source material:
- Real metrics from the user replace estimated [VERIFY] markers
- User-provided context shapes how bullets are framed
- Credibility gaps get addressed with actual evidence
- Hidden achievements get added as new bullets
- Career narrative answers inform the summary and story arc

The Change Log Table in Phase 6 should note which changes were informed by Phase 5 answers vs. which relied on estimation.
